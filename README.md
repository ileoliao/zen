# Harbor Zen

> *A sanctuary for developers to find their flow state.*

## Philosophy

In the modern digital world, we are constantly bombarded with notifications, visual clutter, and cognitive overload. **Harbor Zen** was born from a simple belief: **to do deep work, you need a deep environment.**

We didn't create another music player. We created a **ritual space** — a digital sanctuary that responds to your natural work rhythm throughout the day.

### The Four States of Work

Our work isn't static. Our energy ebbs and flows. Harbor Zen recognizes four distinct phases of a developer's day:

**🌅 Begin (8:00 - 10:00)**  
The gentle awakening. Soft piano notes ease you into the day without jarring your senses. This is your warm-up phase — time to settle in and prepare your mind.

**🎯 Deep Work (10:00 - 13:00)**  
Peak focus hours. A curated blend of piano and guitar melodies creates a cocoon of concentration. The music becomes invisible, allowing your thoughts to flow uninterrupted.

**🌊 Flow (13:00 - 17:00)**  
Sustained productivity. The afternoon demands endurance. These tracks maintain your momentum without overstimulation, carrying you through complex problem-solving.

**🌙 Unwind (17:00 - 19:00)**  
Gentle closure. As the workday ends, softer tones help you transition out of work mode, reflecting on accomplishments and releasing tension.

### Design Principles

**1. Radical Simplicity**
- No playlists to manage
- No endless scrolling
- No visual clutter
- Just one button and your intention

**2. Time Awareness**
The app automatically detects your local time and selects the appropriate scene. Your environment should match your natural circadian rhythm, not fight against it.

**3. Invisible Interface**
After 4 seconds of inactivity, all text fades away. Only the breathing play button remains — pulsing gently to the rhythm of your music. When you need control, tap anywhere. Otherwise, it's just you and your work.

**4. Music Visualization**
The play button doesn't just pulse — it breathes with your music. Real-time audio analysis drives the visual feedback:
- **Bass frequencies** shift the color hue subtly
- **Overall intensity** controls the scale and glow
- **Mid-high frequencies** adjust brightness

This creates a living, responsive environment that feels alive without being distracting.

**5. Gesture-First Interaction**
- **Tap center**: Play/Pause
- **Swipe left/right**: Switch between scenes
- **Long press**: Volume control
- **Anywhere on screen**: Reveal UI

No tiny buttons to hunt for. Your entire screen is the control surface.

## Why No Playlists?

Traditional music players force you to make decisions: what to play, what mood you're in, what's next. These micro-decisions fragment your attention.

Harbor Zen eliminates choice paralysis. Each time period has its own carefully curated soundtrack. When you swipe to change scenes, the music starts automatically. No browsing. No choosing. Just flow.

## The Color Philosophy

Each scene has a color temperature that reflects its purpose:

- **Warm Gold** (Begin): The morning sun, gentle awakening
- **Cool Blue** (Deep Work): Calm focus, clear thinking
- **Soft Green** (Flow): Sustained energy, natural rhythm
- **Deep Purple** (Unwind): Evening reflection, restful closure

These colors aren't just aesthetic — they're functional cues that prime your brain for the type of work ahead.

## Usage

1. Open `index.html` in any modern browser
2. Place your audio files in the `music/` directory
3. The app automatically detects your current time and selects the appropriate scene
4. Tap the center button to start
5. Swipe left or right to change scenes manually
6. Let the music guide your work rhythm

## Technical Notes

- Uses Web Audio API for real-time frequency analysis
- Responsive design works on desktop and mobile
- Touch and mouse support
- Keyboard shortcuts: Space (play/pause), Arrow keys (switch scenes)
